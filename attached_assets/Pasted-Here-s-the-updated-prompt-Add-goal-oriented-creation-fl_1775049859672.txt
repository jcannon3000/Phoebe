The core shift
Currently Eleanor asks people to set a milestone (3 days, 7 days, 14 days) as an afterthought near the end of the creation flow. This undersells the product. The research shows that what people need is not a reminder system — it is a commitment device. Eleanor should position itself from the first step as the thing that makes a specific intention actually happen.
The creation flow should lead with the goal — what you want to do together and how consistently — and then work backwards to the logistics. The specific day and time are details Eleanor handles. The intention is what the user owns.

The distinction this creates
The user’s job is to set the intention. Eleanor’s job is everything else. The specific day, the calendar coordination, the scheduling friction, the follow-up when life gets in the way — all of that belongs to Eleanor. What belongs to the user is the commitment: we want to gather biweekly for three months. Not: we are going to meet on Tuesday mornings. That is a detail. Eleanor handles details. The user owns the goal.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
