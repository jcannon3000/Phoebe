Please redesign the app’s branding, UI copy, and user experience around a new core metaphor:

The app is named Eleanor.
Eleanor is a warm, intelligent personal assistant whose role is to help users cultivate community the way a gardener cultivates a garden. The app helps people plant rituals, tend them over time, and grow them into traditions.

## Core brand idea
This is not a productivity app or a generic scheduling app. It should feel like a gentle, beautiful, modern ritual assistant centered on:
- cultivating community
- planting recurring habits
- tending relationships
- growing traditions over time

The emotional tone should be:
- warm
- calm
- thoughtful
- encouraging
- grounded
- human

Avoid anything that feels:
- corporate
- gamified in a cheap way
- overly cute or childish
- productivity-hustle oriented

Think:
- elegant
- minimal
- slightly poetic
- emotionally resonant
- modern consumer app

## Product concept
Users create recurring social rituals with friends, such as:
- weekly run club
- monthly dinner
- Saturday tennis
- Sunday walk
- coworking mornings

Eleanor helps them:
- set the intention
- choose a cadence
- coordinate schedules
- confirm plans
- keep the ritual going over time
- track streaks in a way that feels like tending growth, not chasing points

## Reframe the UX around gardening language
Please update the interface language and experience using a gardening / cultivation metaphor, but do it with restraint and taste. Do not over-theme every single word. It should feel subtle and premium.

Examples of vocabulary shifts:
- Create group → Plant a Circle
- Start habit / recurring event → Plant a Ritual
- Progress → Growth
- Streak → Bloom or Growth Streak
- Reminder / nudge → Gentle nudge from Eleanor
- Goal → What do you want to grow?
- Community / group → Circle
- Milestone → In Bloom
- Activity history → Garden Journal or Growth Log
- Dashboard / home → Your Garden
- Setup flow → Planting a new ritual
- Success state → This ritual is taking root
- Missed week → Needs tending

Use good judgment. Keep the language beautiful but clear. Do not make the app feel confusing.

## UI/UX direction
Redesign the interface so it feels like a modern app inspired by the idea of a personal garden for relationships and shared rituals.

### Visual direction
- clean, calm, premium mobile UI
- soft organic feeling
- rounded cards and gentle spacing
- subtle natural visual cues
- modern typography
- restrained use of earthy / natural tones
- avoid cheesy illustrations unless they are very elegant and minimal
- light animations can suggest growth, blooming, or tending over time

### Imagery / motifs
Use subtle motifs like:
- seeds
- sprouts
- growth rings
- circles
- sunlight
- pathways
- gardens
- seasonal change

Do not make it look like a children’s gardening app. It should still feel like a serious consumer product for adults.

## Experience changes to implement

### 1. Home screen
Redesign the home screen as Your Garden.

It should show:
- active circles / rituals
- upcoming plans
- current growth / streaks
- rituals that need tending
- encouraging copy from Eleanor

Example tone:
- “Your Thursday Run Crew is taking root.”
- “Your monthly dinner has bloomed for 3 months.”
- “It’s a good week to tend your Sunday walk.”

### 2. Create flow
Redesign the “create group / recurring event” flow into a guided experience called Plant a Ritual.

Flow should include:
- What do you want to grow?
- Choose the type of ritual (run, dinner, tennis, walk, etc.)
- Who is in your circle?
- How often should this happen?
- Preferred days / times
- Optional location
- Short intention or note

When completed, the confirmation should feel ceremonial and satisfying, like planting something meaningful.

### 3. Circle detail page
Each circle should feel like its own living thing.

Show:
- circle name
- members
- next gathering
- cadence
- current growth streak
- attendance / follow-through
- history of past gatherings
- notes or memories from previous meetups

Possible language:
- “Planted 6 weeks ago”
- “Blooming steadily”
- “Needs tending this week”

### 4. Streaks and progress
Replace generic streak visuals with a more meaningful growth system.

Ideas:
- a ritual slowly sprouts and grows over time
- milestones unlock visual stages of growth
- progress is shown as something living and sustained
- missed gatherings don’t feel punitive, but clearly show that care is needed

This should preserve the motivational power of streaks while making it feel warm and relational.

### 5. Notifications and assistant voice
Eleanor should feel like a calm guide.

Rewrite notifications and assistant copy in Eleanor’s voice:
- gentle
- encouraging
- never pushy
- slightly poetic but still practical

Examples:
- “Your Tuesday run is coming up. Ready to keep it growing?”
- “It’s been a little while since your circle gathered.”
- “This ritual is ready to bloom again.”

### 6. Reflection / memory layer
Add or redesign a lightweight reflection layer after each gathering.

After a ritual, allow users to:
- mark that they showed up
- add a note or memory
- optionally upload a photo
- reflect on how it felt

This should go into a Garden Journal or similar archive, reinforcing that the app is helping users cultivate a life, not just manage logistics.
