This reframe is beautiful and it actually deepens the product significantly.

You've just moved Shared Moments from a photo-sharing mechanic into something closer to a shared contemplative practice. The coffee photo is one expression of it. But someone doing five minutes of climate meditation and answering "what came up for you today" is a completely different register of intimacy — and arguably more meaningful. You're not just proving you showed up. You're sharing what the moment meant.

This also aligns the product directly with the How We Gather research in a way the photo-only version didn't fully capture. Ter Kuile and Thurston found that the communities with deepest belonging combined presence with reflection — you showed up AND you made meaning together. The logging options let each circle define what showing up means for them.

Here's the updated prompt for just this feature:

---

**Update the Shared Moments feature — logging types and moment intention**

Do not rebuild what already exists. Update the planting flow and posting page to support multiple logging types and a moment intention.

---

**The core addition**

When creating a Shared Moment, the organizer defines two things that didn't exist before:

**The intention** — a short description of what this moment is and why it matters. This appears on every member's posting page when the window opens. It sets the tone. It is the thing everyone holds together.

**The logging type** — how members record their participation. The organizer chooses one of four options at creation. Every member uses the same logging type for that Shared Moment.

---

**Add to the planting flow — Step 1.5 after naming**

After the name step and before the time step, insert:

**"What is this moment about? 🌿"**

Subtitle in muted text:
"This will appear for your circle when the window opens. It sets the intention."

Large text area, generous padding, warm cream background.

Placeholder text cycling gently between examples:
*"Share your morning coffee together ☕"*
*"Five minutes of stillness before the day starts 🌿"*
*"Breathe together. Pray together. Show up together."*
*"A moment of gratitude, wherever you are 🌸"*
*"Walk outside and notice something beautiful 🚶"*

Character limit: 140 characters. Intentionally short — this is a prompt not an essay.

This field is required. Every Shared Moment needs an intention.

---

**New step in planting flow — "How will your circle log this?"**

Insert between the time step and the goal step.

Header: **"How will your circle show up? 📷"**
Subtitle: "Choose how members log their moment. Everyone uses the same format."

Four large selectable cards:

**📷 Photo**
"A photo of the moment — coffee cup, morning light, wherever you are."
Best for: coffee rituals, walks, meals, anything visual

**✍️ Reflection**
"A short written response to a prompt you set."
Best for: prayer, meditation, gratitude, climate moments
On select: reveals a secondary input field:
  "What's your prompt?"
  Placeholder: "How was your experience today?"
  "What are you grateful for right now?"
  "What did you notice in your five minutes?"
  "What came up for you?"
  Character limit: 100 characters

**📷 + ✍️ Both**
"A photo and a short reflection — presence and meaning together."
Best for: circles that want the image and the words

**✅ Just show up**
"No photo, no words. Just mark that you were here."
Best for: meditation, prayer, breathing practices where the doing is enough

**Update the journal**

Each journal entry now shows the logging type and content appropriately.

Photo entry: photo displayed full width
Reflection entry: prompt shown in italic, responses below in warm cards — each member's reflection, no names attached, just the words
Both entry: photo grid with reflections beneath each
Check-in entry: presence dots only — [X of Y] showed up — clean and simple

The reflection journal is particularly powerful over time. Months of responses to "what are you grateful for right now" from a circle of friends, accumulated, becomes an extraordinary shared document of a friendship. Not a chat history. Not a photo album. A record of meaning-making done together.

---

**Tone notes for reflection prompts**

The reflection prompt the organizer sets should be open and generous not extractive. Suggest these examples in the UI as inspiration:

For contemplative moments:
*"What came up for you today?"*
*"What did you notice in your five minutes?"*
*"What are you carrying right now?"*

For gratitude rituals:
*"What are you grateful for in this moment?"*
*"Name one small thing that was good today."*

For climate and justice practices:
*"What did you hold in your heart today?"*
*"What gave you hope this week?"*
*"What are you called to tend?"*

For social moments:
*"How was your coffee? ☕"*
*"Where are you right now?"*
*"What's one word for your morning?"*

Display three rotating examples in muted italic beneath the reflection prompt input field during creation so organizers feel the range of possibility.

---

**Update the Shared Moments section on circle detail**

For reflection and both type moments, show the most recent reflection from each member in the feed — truncated to one line, full text on tap. Warm card style, no names attached to specific reflections (intentional — the anonymity makes sharing feel safer and the collective voice more powerful).

For check-in type moments, show a simple presence record: date, how many showed up, gentle and clean.

---

**Do not build**

Comments or replies on reflections
Likes or reactions on any post type
Visible attribution of specific reflections to specific members (anonymity is intentional)
Any feature not listed above

**The one thing to get right**

The intention text is the soul of the feature. It appears in the calendar notification. It appears at the top of the posting page. It appears in the journal. It is what makes this more than a photo app or a check-in tool. It answers the question: why are we doing this together? Every Shared Moment has an answer to that question. Build every screen so the intention is the first thing a member encounters.

Someone opening their calendar at 7am, seeing "Five minutes of stillness before the day starts 🌿" in an Eleanor event, tapping the link, landing on a page that shows them that same intention and a quiet text field asking "what did you notice in your five minutes" — that is a different product from anything else in the market. That is Eleanor.
