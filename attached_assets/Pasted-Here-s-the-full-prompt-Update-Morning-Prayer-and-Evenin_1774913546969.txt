WHAT THESE TWO PRACTICES ARE
Morning Prayer and Evening Prayer are the two Daily Offices of the Book of Common Prayer. They are not configurable spiritual practices — they are specific liturgical offices with a defined structure, defined content, and a defined time of day. Morning Prayer is always prayed in the morning. Evening Prayer is always prayed in the evening. Eleanor does not ask users to customize them. The BCP has already done that.
What Eleanor does is hold the commitment and the companionship. When two or more people commit to praying Morning Prayer or Evening Prayer together through Eleanor, they are making a promise to pray the same words from the same book at the same time of day — each in their own home, each at their own schedule — knowing the other is doing the same. This is decentralized fellowship. Not in the same room. Together in spirit.
This language — together in spirit, praying the same words, decentralized fellowship — should be felt throughout both flows.

THE BCP REFERENCE
Eleanor always points members to their physical Book of Common Prayer first and to the online BCP second.
Morning Prayer Rite II begins on page 75 of the Book of Common Prayer.
Evening Prayer Rite II begins on page 115 of the Book of Common Prayer.
For those without a physical BCP:
Morning Prayer Rite II online: bcponline.org/MP2.html
Evening Prayer Rite II online: bcponline.org/EP2.html
These references appear in every calendar invite, the posting page, and the confirmation screen. Always show both — physical page number first, online link second.
