Here’s the Replit prompt to reimagine it:

Reimagine the Garden dashboard — timeline view
Do not change the underlying data or any other screens. Only redesign the home dashboard (Your Garden).

The new concept
The dashboard is a living timeline — not a list of circles or rituals to manage, but a chronological feed of what has happened, what is happening now, and what is coming up. Like a Strava activity feed but for your relationships. The user scrolls through time. Past moments that bloomed. The current window if one is open. Upcoming gatherings and moment windows.
Every event on the timeline is either a Gathering (in-person) or a Shared Moment (decentralized ritual). They look visually distinct at a glance. No confusion between the two.

Remove completely
	∙	“Ready to plant” label — never show this on an active ritual
	∙	Circle-based card grouping — the timeline is event-based not circle-based
	∙	“Just planted” as a primary label — move growth state to a subtle secondary position

Timeline structure
The feed has three sections flowing continuously, no hard section breaks, just subtle time anchors:

↑ PAST — what bloomed and what withered
  (most recent first, scrollable upward history)

NOW — if a window is currently open, pinned here

↑ COMING UP — next 7 days of scheduled events
  (soonest first)


Each section has a small floating time anchor label — muted, small, left-aligned:

Yesterday
Today
This week


These appear as sticky labels as you scroll, like a Strava feed date divider. Subtle, not heavy.

Two card types — visually distinct
Gathering card — in-person

Status states:
	∙	Upcoming: shows time until window opens

	∙	Completed (bloomed): sage bar + “🌸 Both showed up — streak counts”
	∙	Solo (one person): muted bar + “🌿 Only you showed up — streak resting”
	∙	Missed: blush bar + “🥀 No one showed up”
The visual distinction between amber left bar (gathering) and sage left bar (shared moment) means at a glance the user reads the timeline without needing to parse text.

Streak display

Past events
Past events show in the feed with reduced opacity — about 70% — so the visual weight naturally pulls toward upcoming and present. Completed ones have a sage tint. Withered ones have a blush tint. Both are readable but clearly historical.
Tapping any past event opens the journal entry for that window or gathering.

The feeling to aim for
Opening Eleanor should feel like opening a garden journal that is also a calendar that is also a Strava feed. You see what bloomed yesterday. You see what’s open right now. You see what’s coming up this week. Everything is alive and in motion. Nothing feels like an object waiting to be managed.
The amber and sage left bars do the work of differentiation without explanation. The timeline does the work of orientation without navigation. The NOW card does the work of urgency without pressure.
It should feel like your relationships are alive and Eleanor is tending them quietly while you live your life.
