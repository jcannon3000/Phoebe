THE CORE DISTINCTION — communicate this everywhere
Eleanor has two ways of tending relationships across distance and time.
Practices are for when you cannot be together. They are spiritual and contemplative rituals — prayer, breath, stillness, intercession — that two or more people do simultaneously from wherever they are. You are not in the same room. But you are doing the same thing, at the same time, holding the same intention. The practice is what keeps the connection alive between gatherings.
Traditions are what bring you together. They are recurring shared activities — a Friday dinner, a monthly walk, a standing coffee — where showing up means actually being in the same place, or at least the same call. Traditions are the gathering itself. They are what Eleanor coordinates so the thing you keep meaning to do actually happens, again and again, until it becomes part of who you are to each other.
This distinction should be felt on every screen in these two flows. Practices feel contemplative, interior, quiet. Traditions feel social, celebratory, anticipatory.
